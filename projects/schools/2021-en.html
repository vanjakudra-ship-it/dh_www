V Edition: "EVELA 2021"

Main successes

The fifth edition gathered a total of 84 participants, of which 32 were from Serbia and 52 international participants from 27 countries, including Romania, Spain, Mexico, Great Britain, the United States of America, Colombia and others. The speakers were 7 academics and experts from the United States of America, Portugal, Croatia, Mexico and Serbia.

  • Location: Online on the Zoom platform
  • Date: July 21 - 25, 2021

The organizer of the project is the Association of Hispanists of Serbia, while the co-organizers are the Institute for European Studies in Belgrade and the Ibero-American Center of the Faculty of Philosophy of the University of Novi Sad.

Activities

As part of the program, on July 20 at the "Parobrod" Cultural Center, an introductory cultural activity was held, a discussion about the work "Open Veins of Latin America" by Eduardo Galeano and the promotion of the first translation into Serbian.

Lecturers and topics

Walter Mignolo (USA): Eastern Europe and Latin America in the colonial horizon of modernity
Andrés Malamud (PT): Regional breakdown in times of pandemic
Branko Anđić (SRB): Literature of the new normality in Latin America
Dejan Mihailović (MX): Latin America: Cognitive Capitalism, Postcolonialism, and the Return of the State
Victor Rivas (CAN): Reverse Resistances: Venezuelan Decolonization in the Face of Colonialism
Aleksandra Peršić (SRB): Workshop: Debt Policies in Latin America and the Caribbean
Vedrana Lovrinović (CRO): Workshop: The female body as a battlefield - femicide and gender violence in Latin American art and literature

The school's activities included online lectures and presentations, which enabled the exchange of knowledge and experiences among participants from different academic and cultural backgrounds.
