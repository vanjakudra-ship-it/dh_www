2018

II Edition: "EVELA 2018"

Main successes

The lecturers in the 2018 edition were: guest of honor Raul Fornet Betancourt, Cuban philosopher, professor of philosophy at the University of Bremen; Dejan Mihailović, professor at the Monterrey Faculty of Technology in Mexico City; Iva Čukić, co-founder of the Ministry of Space; Nemanja Džuverović, assistant professor at Peace Studies at the Faculty of Political Sciences in Belgrade. We were also honored to host Professor Alexis Toribio Dantas from the State University of Rio de Janeiro, who contributed to the Summer School with his interventions on the topic of violence in Brazil.

  • Location: Balgrade and Novi Sad
  • Date: July 10 - 12, 2018

Institutions that have been supporting us for two years are: Institute for European Studies, Institut Cervantes, YULATAM, Andrzej Dembicz Foundation, Faculty of Philology in Belgrade, Faculty of Philosophy in Novi Sad, Alteraktiva, and the following institutions joined them: Brazilian Embassy, ​​Mediterran Publishing, Peruvian Super Foods, Faculty of Political Sciences in Zagreb, Institute for Interdisciplinary and Intercultural Research and JungleTribe. Institutional support is one of the indicators of the importance of the project and its size in the region.

Activities

An important factor that emphasizes the effort and importance of the development of Latin American studies in Serbia is the presence of our guest of honor Raúl Fornet Betancourt in the media. Three interviews were published (Politika, Danas and Večernje novosti), as well as two television shows (Oko magazin and Kulturni dnevnik) with his participation.

Lecturers and topics

Raúl Fornet Betancourt and Dejan Mihailović: Latin American thought in the post-globalization period
Raúl Fornet Betancourt: History and tasks of philosophy in Latin America
Nemanja Džuverović, Iva Čukić, Dejan Mihailović: The Age of Violence: A Latin American Perspective
Raúl Fornet Betancourt: Intercultural philosophy: philosophy for better coexistence

This year's edition of the Summer School was dedicated to intercultural philosophy, as the original theme. The philosophical thread is the link that connects all events within the School, establishing last year's themes that were based on the search for utopia, the period of globalization and Latin American identity.
