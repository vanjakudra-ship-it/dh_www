IV Edition: "EVELA 2020"

Main successes

The fourth edition brought together 132 participants from Serbia, Romania, Spain, Italy, United Arab Emirates, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Poland, Hungary, Costa Rica, Mexico, United Kingdom, Germany, Croatia, Brazil, Thailand, Austria, United States of America, France, El Salvador, Holland, Cuba, Madagascar, Peru, Barbados, Montenegro, Ecuador, Colombia, and the Czech Republic. Ten lecturers from Portugal, Argentina, Brazil, Serbia, Romania, Spain, Mexico, and Austria participated.

  • Location: Online on the Zoom platform
  • Date: August 12 - 16, 2020

The success of the International Summer School of Latin American Studies was made possible by the generous support of several esteemed institutions. We extend our gratitude to the Ministry of Culture of the Republic of Serbia, the Ibero-American Center at the Faculty of Philosophy in Novi Sad, the Institute for European Studies in Serbia, the Professor Andrzej Dembicz Foundation from Poland, the Institute for Intercultural and Comparative Research IDEAZ from Austria, NUCLEAS from Brazil, and the Cervantes Institute in Serbia.

Activities

In addition to lectures, two presentations were held. The first one was by Pablo Vommaro, Director of Research at CLASCO, entitled "The importance of sharing experiences and building networks - CLACSO". The second one was by Bojana Kovačević Petrović, President at CIBAM, entitiled "CIBAM Presentation".

Lecturers and topics

Luis A. Fretes Carreras (PT): Geopolitics of Latin America in times of uncertainty
Maria Teresa Toribio Brittes Lemos (BRA): Reflections on inequalities and exclusions in Latin America
Ilinca Ilian (RO): Between maternophobia, maternolatry, and misiopedia: stances on the filial relationship in contemporary Latin American literature
Nikolina Židek (ESP): Disobedient Stories with Spelling Errors: A New Actor in the Argentine Quest for Truth, Memory, and Justice
Dejan Mihailović (MX): Cognitive capitalism: new forms of domination, resistance, and future scenarios
Johannes Maerk (AUT): Neoliberalism in times of Covid-19. Perspectives from Latin America and Europe
Silvia Izquierdo de Todorović (SRB): The literature of the Peruvian Amazon and literature inspired by the Amazon
Carlos A. Pérez Ricart (MX): Arms trafficking in Latin America: a challenge to security in the world's most violent region

The fourth edition faced challenges imposed by the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. However, simultaneously, it gathered the largest number of participants from all continents, united in their sharing of ideas and knowledge about Latin America.
