2019

III Edition: "EVELA 2019, Latin America and the Caribbean, why do we need to know more?"

Main Achievements

The third edition brought together 18 participants from Serbia, Tunisia, Russia, and the United Kingdom. 12 academics and experts from different fields related to Latin America and the Caribbean participated, from both Serbia and Mexico, Argentina, Brazil, Costa Rica, Spain, the United States, Germany, and Austria.

  • Locations: Belgrade and Novi Sad
  • Date: June 29 - July 3, 2019

Institutions that supported the project: Institute for European Studies, Cervantes Institute, Cultural Center "Stari Grad", Faculty of Philosophy at the University of Novi Sad, Embassy of Spain, University Business Academy in Novi Sad, daily newspaper Danas, Andrzej Dembicz Foundation (Poland), Institute for Intercultural and Comparative Research IDEAZ (Austria), NUCLEAS (Brazil), International School of Intercultural Philosophy ISIS (Spain), Faculty of Philology at the University of Belgrade, AlternAktiva, YULATAM, Mediterran Publishing House Novi Sad, Self-Education University "Svetozar Marković".

Media coverage of the school: several event posts on social media, 2 radio broadcasts, 6 publications in newspapers and magazines.

Activities

Four introductory conversations were held before the start of the school, which were open to the public: Influences and perspectives on neo-Zapatism, Veselina Gacinska and Sergio García García; Confronting the past in Argentina and Spain: unfinished stories, Nikolina Židek; The role of South American states in United Nations peacekeeping operations, Kai Michael Kenkel; Argentine literature today: what is written, edited, read, Edgardo Scott and Guido Herzovich.

Two round tables were also held during the school. The first was held at the Cervantes Institute: Latin America and the Caribbean: why do we need to know more? Participants: Carlos Juárez Centeno (Argentina), Dejan Mihailović (Mexico), Emilio Gallardo Saborido (Spain), Johannes Merk (Austria), Dalibor Soldatić (Serbia), Moderator: Tijana Čupić (Serbia). The second was held at the Institute for European Studies: Latin America facing a new wave of changes: between Trump and new leaders. Participants: Dejan Mihailović (Mexico), Alexis Toribio Dantas (Brazil), Óscar Barbosa Lizano (Costa Rica), Moderator: Miša Đurković (Serbia).

Four workshops were also held: Epistemology of the South, Dejan Mihailović; The importance of politics in individual education - Claudio Naranjo and his vision of education as a means for personal and social development, Ana Jovanović; Contemporary relations between Cuba and Eastern Europe, Emilio Gallardo Saborido; Microstory: literary genre of the new Hispanic American millennium, Bojana Kovačević Petrović.

There were also two cultural activities open to the public: The Yu Mex Phenomenon - A day in the life in Yugoslav music and film (participants: Aleksandra Đorđević, Francisco Ohem, moderator: Jelena Spasojević), as well as a pub quiz about Latin America and the Caribbean.

Lecturers and Topics

Adolfo Alberto Laborde Carranco (MX): Mexico's foreign policy in the context of the "4T" (fourth transformation)
Alexis Toribio Dantas (BRA): Perspectives of the Brazilian economy
Višeslav Simić (MX): Foreign policy possibilities of Latin American countries outside the U.S. Monroe Doctrine
Johannes Merk (AUT): Global South perspective. Epistemological questions related to Latin America and the Caribbean
Gorica Majstorović (USA): The influence of Mexican muralism on art, literature, and contemporary communication networks
Guido Herzovich (ARG): What can Argentine cinema teach us about neoliberalism?
Sanja Savkić (DE): The importance of the Aztec Great Temple as a driver of power, identity, and establishing relationships with others
Tijana Bakić Hajden (ARG): Latin American feminism: practical application, theories, debates, and public awareness about it
Carlos Juárez Centeno (ARG): South America's position in the international context through the prism of new leaders in its states, ideological shifts, and uncertainties facing the continent
Óscar Barbosa Lizano (POL): The Caribbean as the "American Mediterranean". Debate between a unipolar and multipolar world

With its concept and quality achieved in the last three years, the Summer School seeks to contribute to empowering young people to try to professionally engage with those regions of the world to which, unjustly, we do not pay enough attention; with which we traditionally and obviously maintain friendly relations; which bring us a large number of solutions and ideas in the face of the challenges of globalization and modern society of the 21st century.
