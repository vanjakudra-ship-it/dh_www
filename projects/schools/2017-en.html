2017

I Edition: "EVELA 2017, Latin America and the challenges of the modern world"

Main Achievements

The first edition brought together 23 participants from Serbia, Montenegro, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Slovenia, Poland, Spain, Argentina, Brazil, Russia and Turkey. Twelve lecturers from Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Poland, Spain, Mexico, Argentina and Australia participated.

  • Location: Belgrade and Novi Sad
  • Date: July 20 - 24, 2017

Institutions that supported the project: Faculty of Philology, University of Belgrade, Faculty of Philosophy, University of Novi Sad, Institute for European Studies, Institute for Philosophy and Social Theory, Cervantes Institute in Belgrade, Professor Andrzej Dembicz Foundation (Warsaw), KC Grad, AlernAktiva, YULATAM.

Media coverage of the school: 3 television shows (presentation of the goals of the Summer School and interviews), 5 radio shows (presentation of the goals of the project, lecturers, reports), 6 publications in newspapers and magazines (mostly interviews).

Activities

Workshops and informal discussions: Contemporary Latin American prose (Bojana Kovaćević Petrović); What does Latin America tell us? Conversation about social movements, politics and psychoanalysis (Igor Štiks).

Professional seminar: Steps towards a transcapitalist life, a seminar dedicated to the special guest of the school, Horacio Ceruti-Guldberg (Mexico)

Cultural events: Promotion of the first Serbian translation of the book by Mexican author Leopoldo Zea, Discovery and Identity of Latin America (participants: Horacio Ceruti-Guldberg, Anđela Gašić; moderator: Jelena Spasojević); Presentation and discussion about Carlos Fuentes' film cycle and book The Buried Mirror (participants: Ángel Esteban del Campo, Dubravka Sužnevič, Bojana Kovaćević Petrović).

Tribune: Comparative analysis of Europe and Latin America (participants: Dejan Mihailović, Dalibor Soldatić, Miša Đurković, Carlos Juárez Centeno; moderator: Đurđa Trajković)

Lecturers and topics

Dejan Mihailović (MX): Latin America and the challenges of the modern world
Katarzyna Dembicz (PL): Multidimensional Latin America: Where to?
Ángel Esteban del Campo (ES): The role of Casa de las Americas in the development of the Latin American boom
Dubravka Sužnjević (MX): Literary translation as a profession, art, and cultural representation. Problems of linguistic differences in the various Spanish-speaking regions and the publishing market.
Diana Palaversić (AUS): The narco-literature boom and Víctor Rascón Banda's Smuggling as a masterpiece
Višeslav Simić (MX): Legal status and rights of human beings crossing state borders in accordance with international and domestic laws in relation to Western and Islamic emotional, socio-cultural and ideological representations and constructs
Horacio Ceruti-Guldberg (MX): Current state of philosophy and Latin American thought facing new challenges
Esther Susana Borgarello (ARG): Right to information within communication media in Latin America and the American Convention on Human Rights
Carlos Juárez Centeno (ARG): Human rights and international law; a Latin American perspective
Óscar Barboza Lizano (PL): Latin American studies today; between identity, history and decoloniality

The Proceedings of the first edition of the International Summer School of Latin American Studies were published online. These bilingual Proceedings (Spanish and Serbian) include 8 articles, 1 interview, 3 commission reviews and 1 translation.
