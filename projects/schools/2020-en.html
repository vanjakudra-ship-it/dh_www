2020

IV Edition: "EVELA 2020"

Main successes

The eighth edition brought together 15 participants from Serbia, Colombia and Ecuador. Nine lecturers from Spain, Italy, Brazil, Chile and Serbia participated.

  • Location: Online on the Zoom platform
  • Date: August 12 - 16, 2020

Organizator: Društvo hispanista; Koorganizatori: Institut za evropske studije, Iberoamerički centar Filozofskog fakulteta Univerziteta u Novom Sadu.

Podržali: Ustanova kulture "Parobrod" iz Beograda, Fakultet za medije i komunikacije iz Srbije, Fondacija profesora Andžeja Dembiča iz Poljske, Institut za interkulturno i uporedno istraživanje IDEAZ iz Austrije, NUCLEAS iz Brazila i pojekat ELASOC iz Španije.

Activities

In addition to lectures, three creative writing workshops were held with Andrea Jeftanović (Chile), and the promotion of two books: "Political systems of Latin American countries" by Rajko Petrović and "Yugoslavia, war and peace in the new Balkan states" by José Ángel Ruiz Jiménez. Bojana Kovačević Petrović promoted the ICA2025 congress. Novi odmetnici presented their music album "Iznad tame (pjesme za Viktora Haru)" dedicated to Víctor Jara, and there was also a pub quiz inspired by Latin America.

Lecturers and topics

Luis A. Fretes Carreras (PT): Geopolitics of Latin America in times of uncertainty
Maria Teresa Toribio Brittes Lemos (BRA): Reflections on inequalities and exclusions in Latin America
Ilinca Ilian (RO): Between maternophobia, maternolatry, and misiopedia: stances on the filial relationship in contemporary Latin American literature
Nikolina Židek (ESP): Disobedient Stories with Spelling Errors: A New Actor in the Argentine Quest for Truth, Memory, and Justice
Dejan Mihailović (MX): Cognitive capitalism: new forms of domination, resistance, and future scenarios
Johannes Maerk (AUT): Neoliberalism in times of Covid-19. Perspectives from Latin America and Europe
Silvia Izquierdo de Todorović (SRB): The literature of the Peruvian Amazon and literature inspired by the Amazon
Carlos A. Pérez Ricart (MX): Arms trafficking in Latin America: a challenge to security in the world's most violent region

The fourth edition faced challenges imposed by the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. However, simultaneously, it gathered the largest number of participants from all continents, united in their sharing of ideas and knowledge about Latin America.
