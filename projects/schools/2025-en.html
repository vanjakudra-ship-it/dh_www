IX Special Edition: "EVELA 2025"

Part of the ICA Congress

The ninth edition of the International Summer School of Latin American Studies was held as part of the 58th International Congress of Americanists within the ICA Junior program in co-organization with the Faculty of Philosophy and the Ibero-American Center (CIBAM) operating under its auspices, the Faculty of Technical Sciences, the Faculty of Natural Sciences and Mathematics, the Faculty of Technology of UNS, and the Association of Hispanists.

  • Location: Novi Sad
  • Date: July 2, 2025

After 17 years of the Association of Hispanists' existence, 8 realized International Summer Schools of Latin American Studies, and dozens of smaller and larger projects and activities, the Association was one of five co-organizers of the 58th International Congress of Americanists, held in Novi Sad from June 30 to July 4.

As part of the congress, we directly contributed to one of the largest academic-cultural-diplomatic events in the country and region. Through the collaboration of involved partners, the most valuable thing was achieved – Novi Sad and Serbia were the world's best possible hosts for over 1400 researchers from 69 countries worldwide, of which more than 90% were visiting Serbia for the first time.

Significance and Contribution

We are particularly proud of having directly contributed to the planning and implementation of the ICA Junior program, which is considered one of the most significant segments of this year's edition of the congress. Realized for the first time in Serbia, this program represents a cornerstone and future of the ICA Congress, as it continues in two years in Medellín, Colombia at the 59th edition.

Special Edition Program

Within the ICA Junior program, the 9th special edition of the International Summer School of Latin American Studies was held at the OPENS facilities in Novi Sad. In changed circumstances, the program had a different format compared to previous years.

In one day, an open and friendly conversation was organized dedicated to the exchange of knowledge, experiences, impressions, and perceptions about social movements, student participation, and contemporary struggles for a more just society.

CONVERSATION PARTICIPANTS

The open conversation featured the following participants:

  • • Jelena Spasojević, President of the Association of Hispanists (Serbia)
  • • Andrés Malamud (Institute of Social Sciences, University of Lisbon)
  • • Erik Alfredo Guerrero Márquez (Morena Political Party, Mexico)
  • • Luka Ratić, student at the Faculty of Philology (University of Belgrade, Serbia)

WORKSHOP

After the conversation, a workshop was held by Andrés Malamud, dedicated to writing popular science texts. During the workshop, participants received practical advice on public communication of academic knowledge.

The special edition of the Summer School confirmed the importance of international academic cooperation and continued the tradition of the Association of Hispanists as a space for dialogue, knowledge exchange, and connection of young researchers.
