About Us

ASSOCIATION OF HISPANISTS (OF SERBIA)

In 2007, a group of Hispanic Philology students founded the Association of Hispanists, with the idea of contributing to the expansion of knowledge about the Ibero-American world in Serbia and maintaining a proactive and critical attitude regarding the position of Ibero-American studies in Serbia and the need to learn the Spanish and Portuguese languages.

The Association of Hispanists acts as kind of non-formal education, aiming to bring together undergraduate and graduate students, as well as young activists from social movements, public officials, and members and managers of non-governmental organizations. Its emphasis on an interdisciplinary and informal approach, along with its multi-sectoral reach, fosters a fruitful dialogue among disciplines, individuals, and institutions. These collaborations allow us to develop cultural, educational, and research projects and offer alternative learning and teaching options.

Jelena Spasojević

One of the founders of the Association of Hispanists and president of the board of directors since 2010; one of the founders and project coordinator of EVELA

Building bridges between Serbia and Ibero-American countries is her main professional mission. Although a philologist by education, her interests are numerous, certainly always interdisciplinary, and her actions are creative and often alternative. She is not strictly an academic type of person, but she likes to cooperate with the academia and contribute to it, especially with students and for students, because she firmly believes that education and strengthening the professional capacities of young people is the only true path that can successfully lead us from the end of one epoch we are currently living in, into the next, which we cannot even get a clear glimpse of at the moment. This is supported by her entire work within the Association of Hispanists since its foundation, as well as the concrete results she achieved, for herself personally, but always for the wider community.

Tijana Čupić

Member of the board of directors; one of the founders and program coordinator of the EVELA project

Tijana Cupic is an Assistant Professor of Instruction at the Modern Languages Department at Ohio University. Her research focuses on contemporary Latin American literature, particularly the affective politics of migration within transnational contexts, including Mexico, Central America, and the Balkans.

Anđela Gašić

Member of the supervisory board; one of the founders and logistics coordinator of EVELA

Angela has a master's degree in Hispanic Studies from the Faculty of Philology at the University of Belgrade. She has been a member of the organizing team of the Summer School of Latin American Studies since the very beginning, first as a coordinator of volunteers, and afterwards she has been in charge of logistics and administration. The Spanish language and Hispanic culture have been her great love since childhood. In addition to formal education, she has extensive professional experience in the recruitment of foreign language teachers, and within the Association of Hispanists, she is particularly motivated by connecting people and ideas and contributing to the development and visibility of the community of Hispanists in Serbia.
